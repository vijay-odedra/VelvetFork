Our Chef

The Vision Behind the Cuisine

Chef Elena Rossi

25 Years of Culinary Excellence

Born in Tuscany and trained in the kitchens of Lyon, Chef Elena Rossi brings a lifetime of culinary tradition to VelvetFork. Her journey began at her grandmother's side, learning the rhythms of the Italian countryside and the importance of honoring each ingredient's story.

After classical training at the Institut Paul Bocuse, Chef Rossi honed her craft in Michelin-starred kitchens across Europe before bringing her vision to North America. Her approach blends technical precision with intuitive creativity, respecting tradition while constantly exploring new expressions of flavor.

At VelvetFork, Chef Rossi leads with quiet confidence, mentoring her team with the same patience and attention to detail that defines her cooking. She believes a restaurant's soul comes from the hands that prepare its food, and this philosophy shapes every dish that leaves her kitchen.

Great cooking begins with listening — to the seasons, to the ingredients, and to the stories they want to tell. My role is simply to guide them to the plate with respect and care.

Culinary Philosophy

For Chef Rossi, cooking is an act of translation — transforming the language of nature into something that speaks directly to the soul. She approaches each ingredient as a partner in conversation, not merely a component to be manipulated.

This philosophy manifests in her commitment to seasonal sourcing, her reverence for traditional techniques, and her belief that restraint often speaks louder than excess. The most memorable dishes, she believes, are those that create space for ingredients to express their essential character.

Her cooking reflects a deep understanding that food is memory, connection, and culture — not just sustenance. Each plate at VelvetFork carries this awareness, creating experiences that linger long after the meal has ended.

Signature Creation

A dish that captures the essence of Chef Rossi's philosophy

Herb-Infused Wild Mushroom Risotto

This dish represents Chef Rossi's approach in its purest form: seasonal foraged mushrooms, Carnaroli rice cooked to exacting standards, Parmigiano-Reggiano aged 36 months, and white truffle oil from Alba. What appears simple on the menu reveals extraordinary complexity in execution.

The rice is toasted slowly to develop nutty flavors before being cooked in a mushroom broth made from dried porcini. Each variety of mushroom is cooked separately to preserve its unique texture and flavor before being folded together at the perfect moment.

"This risotto taught me that true sophistication lies in understanding when to step back and let ingredients speak for themselves. It's not about what I add, but what I choose not to add."
